Bronchial Asthma

Chronic inflammatory disorder with airway hyper responsiveness characterized by variability of symptoms (wheeze, cough, shortness of breath and chest tightness) and proven reversibility with bronchodilators.

Acute severe asthma Life threatening asthma
Respiratory rate > 30/min Central cyanosis
Pulse rate >110/min Silent chest
Prominent accessory muscles of respiration Confusion, coma
Unable to speak in sentences (cannot complete one sentence in a breath) Cannot speak
Bilateral rhonchi Bradycardia, hypotension
PEFR < 50% of expected PEFR< 33% of expected
Investigations
  1. CBC,
  2. RFT, RBS,
  3. Chest X ray
  4. ECG
  5. Sputum profile
  6. ABG (for severe causes)
