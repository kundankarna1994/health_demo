Prostate Cancer
Prostate cancer is the most common type of cancer in men in the United States (other than skin cancer). On average, one man in nine will be diagnosed with prostate cancer during his lifetime; however, only 17% of men diagnosed with prostate cancer will die from it.
Other Information
  • What is Prostate Cancer?
    Billions of cells make up the human body and sometimes these cells reproduce in an uncontrolled way, forming a lump of tissue that may be described as a tumor.
  • What causes Prostate Cancer?
    Although doctors do not know what causes prostate cancer, they do know that testosterone stimulates prostate cancer growth.
  • Can Prostate Cancer Be Found Early?
    Screening for prostate cancer is a little bit controversial because experts are unsure whether the benefits of testing all men for prostate cancer outweigh the risks, such as finding and treating cancers that probably would have never caused any problems.
  • Symptoms of Prostate Cancer
    Some prostate cancers are so small that they cannot be felt during a routine rectal examination and may not cause any symptoms.
